# The UK's First DfE-Aligned AI Risk & Readiness Benchmark for Schools

## The Problem

Schools are under increasing pressure to adopt AI safely, but most guidance tells schools what they should do rather than helping them understand their actual level of exposure.

School leaders are asking:

* Are our teachers becoming over-reliant on AI?
* Are students still developing independent thinking?
* Do parents understand the risks and opportunities?
* Are we compliant with DfE, ICO, KCSIE, JCQ and Ofqual guidance?
* Do we have the right governance, policies and safeguards in place?

Most existing solutions focus on AI adoption.

Very few measure AI dependency, human oversight, behavioural risk and governance maturity across the whole school community.

# The Eight Benchmark Domains

## 1. Safe Adoption

Does the school assess risks before introducing AI?

## 2. Human Oversight

Are users reviewing, challenging and verifying AI outputs?

## 3. AI Dependency

How reliant have users become on AI?

## 4. Privacy & Data Protection

Are staff and students protecting personal information?

## 5. Safeguarding

Are AI-related safeguarding risks understood and managed?

## 6. Assessment Integrity

Are assessments protected against inappropriate AI use?

## 7. AI Literacy

Do users understand AI capabilities and limitations?

## 8. Governance

Does the school have policies, accountability and oversight?

# Positioning Statement

The UK's first DfE-aligned AI Risk & Readiness Benchmark for Schools.

Measure AI Dependency, Human Oversight, Governance, Safeguarding, Privacy and Assessment Integrity across Teachers, Students, Parents and School Leaders.

Helping schools adopt AI safely, responsibly and with confidence.
Create a WordPress plugin called “AI Risk & Readiness Benchmark”.

Goal:
Build a DfE-aligned AI Risk & Readiness Benchmark tool for UK schools. It should work inside WordPress via shortcode, e.g. [ai_risk_benchmark]. The site owner uses WordPress as the CMS, so all text, questions and recommendations should be editable in the WordPress admin.

Core users:
1. Teachers
2. Students
3. Parents
4. School Leaders

Each user group should complete a mini audit. The audit should calculate risk/readiness scores across these domains:

1. Safe Adoption
2. Human Oversight
3. AI Dependency
4. Privacy & Data Protection
5. Safeguarding
6. Assessment Integrity
7. AI Literacy
8. Governance

Important positioning:
This is not legal advice. It is an educational DfE-aligned self-assessment tool for schools in England. It should reference DfE, ICO, KCSIE, JCQ, Ofqual and Ofsted guidance, but not claim official endorsement.

Main outputs:
After completion, show:
- Overall DfE AI Alignment Score
- AI Dependency Index

- Governance Maturity Score

Use radio buttons, sliders and select fields.

Example school leader questions:

- Are staff trained on AI risks?
- Are AI risks included in safeguarding procedures?
- Has the school reviewed AI use against data protection requirements?
- Are assessments reviewed for AI exposure?

Scoring:
Each answer should map to a numeric risk score from 0 to 3.
0 = low risk / strong readiness
1 = mild risk
2 = moderate risk
3 = high risk

Calculate:
domain_score = average score for questions in that domain
risk_percentage = domain_score / 3 * 100

Invert where needed for readiness:
readiness_percentage = 100 - risk_percentage

Bands:
0-30 = Low
31-60 = Moderate
61-80 = High
81-100 = Critical

For Human Oversight Ratio:
Use the answer to “What percentage of AI output do you typically modify?”
0-10% = Critical reliance
11-25% = High reliance
26-50% = Moderate oversight
51%+ = Strong oversight
